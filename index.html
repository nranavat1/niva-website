Refining Human Pose Estimation
2025
Computer Vision Pose Estimation Deep Learning Vision Transformer

While modern pose estimation models such as ViTPose achieve strong performance on static keypoint detection, accurately predicting future human motion remains challenging due to temporal complexity and variability in human behavior. To address this, I built a human pose estimation and future pose prediction pipeline using a ViTPose backbone with a Feature Pyramid Network (FPN) to capture multi-scale spatial features and improve keypoint detection. The system was trained on approximately 14,000 pose sequences, using temporal windows of past frames to predict future joint trajectories. An initial baseline captured coarse motion trends but struggled with fine-grained joint accuracy and temporal consistency over longer prediction horizons. By refining the model with Smooth L1 Loss and extended training, I improved stability and alignment with ground-truth motion, resulting in more consistent frame-to-frame predictions. This work explores how to move beyond static pose estimation toward robust temporal modeling of human motion under real-world variability.

Bus Stop Safety Evaluator
2025
Computer Vision Vision Language Models Object Detection

School bus stops are not static and must adapt to changing environments, but there is currently no scalable way to evaluate their safety in real time, especially in scenarios involving children and dynamic traffic conditions. To address this, I developed a multimodal perception system that assesses the safety of potential and existing bus stops using a structured threat vector model. The pipeline combines YOLO-based object detection to identify vehicles and scene-level risks with a fine-tuned Gemini vision-language model (VLM) to extract higher-level context such as sidewalk presence, visibility, and environmental conditions from panoramic imagery. These signals are fused with geospatial data, including OpenStreetMap features and historical crash records, to quantify risk across traffic, pedestrian, and environmental dimensions. The system produces a unified safety score that enables scalable evaluation and comparison of bus stop locations. This work focuses on applying perception models to real-world, safety-critical decision making under dynamic and uncertain conditions.
